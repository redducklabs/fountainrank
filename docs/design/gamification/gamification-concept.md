# FountainRank Gamification Concept

## Goal

Encourage people to add, verify, rate, and improve public drinking fountain data, especially while FountainRank is new and the map is still sparse.

The core idea: make participation feel like useful exploration, not busywork. Rewards should emphasize real-world contribution quality over raw volume.

## Recommended Theme: Water Scouts

Users help map public water access by becoming local "Water Scouts." The app rewards useful field contributions: finding fountains, confirming they still work, rating quality, adding photos, and filling coverage gaps.

This theme fits the product without making the app feel childish. It gives users a clear identity: they are helping build a better public water map.

## Core Loop

1. User opens the map and sees nearby missing or under-verified areas.
2. User adds, rates, verifies, or photographs a fountain.
3. The app gives immediate feedback: points, badge progress, local impact, and contribution streak.
4. The contribution improves the public map.
5. User sees progress on personal, neighborhood, and city goals.

## Contribution Points

Award points for actions that improve data quality:

- Add a new fountain: high points
- Add the first fountain in an unmapped area: bonus points
- Verify a fountain is still there and working: medium points
- Rate a fountain across clarity, taste, pressure, and appearance: medium points
- Add a useful photo: medium points
- Update stale information: medium points
- Report a duplicate, broken fountain, or bad listing: small points after review

Avoid rewarding unlimited repeat actions. For example, rating the same fountain repeatedly should not farm points. Edits can improve the map, but only meaningful verified changes should count.

## Early-Stage Mechanics

### Founder Badges

Limited early badges create urgency without requiring prizes:

- Founding Scout: joined during the early launch period
- First Fountain: added your first public fountain
- Neighborhood Founder: added the first fountain in a local area
- City Starter: helped create the first useful set of fountains in a city
- Original 100: among the first 100 contributors

These badges should remain visible on profiles later, so early users feel their contribution mattered.

### Coverage Quests

Create location-based quests that guide users toward useful actions:

- Add 3 fountains near parks
- Verify 5 fountains near downtown
- Rate fountains near transit stops
- Help map a campus, trail, or neighborhood

In the initial stage, quests should be generated around practical coverage gaps instead of arbitrary tasks.

### Map Progress

Show local progress in a way that makes sparse data feel like an opportunity:

- "Your area has 2 mapped fountains"
- "3 more verified fountains will complete this neighborhood"
- "You added the first fountain here"
- "This fountain needs its first rating"

This gives people a reason to contribute even before there is a dense leaderboard.

### First-Rater Bonus

Give a bonus when a user is the first person to rate or verify an existing fountain. This encourages improvement of early listings, not just adding new pins.

### Local Leaderboards

Use leaderboards carefully. Prioritize local and contribution-quality rankings:

- Top scouts near you
- Most helpful contributors this month
- Most fountains verified
- Most new areas mapped

Avoid a single global leaderboard as the primary motivator, because early global boards can become discouraging or easy to dominate.

## Badge Ideas

- Hydrated Helper: submitted your first rating
- Pressure Tester: rated pressure on 10 fountains
- Taste Tracker: rated taste on 10 fountains
- Clear Water Club: rated clarity on 10 fountains
- Photo Proof: added your first fountain photo
- Field Verifier: confirmed 10 fountains are working
- Fix Finder: reported a broken fountain
- Trail Mapper: added fountains near parks or trails
- Campus Mapper: helped map fountains around a school or campus
- Civic Mapper: added fountains in public buildings or civic spaces
- Neighborhood Anchor: added the first fountain in an unmapped area

## Data Quality Safeguards

Gamification can create bad incentives, so the system should reward quality:

- Cap repeated points from the same fountain.
- Give bigger rewards after another user confirms a contribution.
- Do not award full points for duplicate fountains.
- Require location proximity for certain actions, such as verification.
- Weight leaderboards by accepted contributions, not raw submissions.
- Track suspicious bursts for moderation review.

## MVP Recommendation

Start with a focused version:

- Points for adding, rating, verifying, and photographing fountains
- Founder badges for early users
- First-fountain and first-rating bonuses
- Local contribution progress on the map
- A profile summary showing points, badges, and contribution counts
- Local leaderboards by area, not just global rank

This is enough to make early participation feel meaningful without building a heavy game system before the core map is proven.

## App Store Copy Angle

FountainRank is not just a map. It is a community challenge to make public water easier to find.

Find fountains, rate what you use, earn badges for helpful contributions, and help your neighborhood become easier to explore with a refillable bottle.
